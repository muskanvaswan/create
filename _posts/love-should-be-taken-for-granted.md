Love, a perplexing emotion, all the world openly claims to misunderstand. Yet, there has been endless discussion on it with a whole ocean of literature. Here I add a drop of my own to it, so bear with me as I confess my romantic desires first.

  

  

I want the kind of love that is matter of factly. The one that is obvious, that leaves no room for guessing or for confusion. There is no question to be asked of whether I’m loved, and whether they’d do this or that for me. They’d do it all, from kisses on my temple to working on our life with me. There is no chase nor push and pull. They’d look at my disbelief of their affection with patient annoyance, confident that someday soon, I’ll manage to start taking their love for granted.

  

  

To some this sounds like a lazy boring idea of love, where there is no challenge, no sparkle. To another this may sound terrifyingly unhealthy, because one must never take the good things in life for granted, right? In plain words, this did sound that way to me too.

  

But I imagine being loved in this manner and I feel a well of possibilities open inside me, not only for the life I may share with this person, but for who I may become. The curiosity to meet this most real version of myself, outweighs the excitement of chase and challenge.

  

I’ve come to realise that we treat love as adventure sports so we can see the most extremist version of ourselves, not the most real. All the epic romances have been that way, full of drama and conflict. But we are no longer at war, there is no society (probably) that is trying to put us on different islands for most of us, then why is love not snapping back to safety?

  

It is true that real is not always good and if we take love for granted without returning a love of the same magnitude, it turns into a dangerous affair. That is why we must strive to be better people not to seduce the next person we are going to love, but so that when we are finally loved in a way where it becomes safe to be just ourselves, the person that comes out is best for the person that loves us so riskily and simply for the world at large.

  

This answered another long standing question of mine — why love? Why are humans so obsessed with it?

  

It’s because when there is enough of it to be taken for granted then perhaps we might all be the realest version of us, and the world would be utopian.
