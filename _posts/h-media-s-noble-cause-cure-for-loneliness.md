I always wrote, but it’s been in a way that I just blurt out the parade of thoughts coursing through my head. Catered to no reader, rarely structured, often dramatic. It always felt inauthentic to change it to make it digestible to an external audience. So my internal monologue and the unbearable need to express it never saw the light of day and never made it out of the Notes app.

  

Lately, a monotony has set in. With too much time on my hands, my thoughts have run rampant, blessing me with a replenished quota of original thoughts and ideas. Along with it came the ache of wanting them to be heard, to be spit out into the world for eyes to see and even just one more soul to relate to. But friends are scarce, and scarcer yet is their time, so woe is me, sitting in a silence full of words and concepts. A silence that is starting to feel more and more like a disservice, like I am a farmer hoarding crops that rot and overflow. The silence hits a crescendo with desperation.

  

It feels like existing without evidence.

  

What would you call this thirst to be heard, to be seen, to be understood, to be observed that I’m most certain every human feels? I call it loneliness. (I know this word is a bit taboo lately, but over time we’ve made every vulnerable part of being human into something so uncool — a tragedy I conveniently try to ignore.)

  

> Eq. 1  
> The need to be observed = loneliness

  

If loneliness is the need to be observed, then art is the mechanism.

  

Everyone has a different definition of art, but I think of it as any expression of meaning. There is art that is meant for limited eyes to see, like intimacy, secrets, or a fight with loved ones. There is art in prayer, its audience your God. Then there are concerts and public performances, a ***one-to-many*** connection meant from one creator for the masses. And then there are jamming sessions and group sports, a ***many-to-many*** expression of art. But all of it needs a witness, and each creator of art seeks and searches for just the right one because **the art (the reality) only exists if observed.** (A subtle nod to quantum philosophy.)

  

The satisfaction achieved by a ***one-to-one*** medium remains unparalleled. The deepest interaction that allows for life-defining emotions like love. Yet it requires a more precise pairing, permutations harder to get right with an increasing volume of candidates. So we must engineer cheaper alternatives, lest the unfortunate population that does not find it die of loneliness.

> Eq. 2  
> The probability of finding an observer–observed pairing is directly proportional to how precise it needs to be (its cardinality).

  

Media, as we know it, allows for the one-to-many model of its expression. A cheaper yet more productive version of the one-to-one expressions of art. It started with one-to-one interactions through messaging apps, and we’re heading to *many-to-many* interactions on the internet, with apps like Clubhouse and more forms of engagement. Media is scaling visibility.

> Eq. 3  
> Media progressively enables increasing cardinality of interactions.

  

Put equations 2 and 3 together and you get: media is reducing the cost of an observer–observed relationship. Now factor in Eq. 1, and we have it: media is making it cheaper to cure loneliness. Well, at least theoretically.

  

But if you superimpose this practically,

We drink “a day in my life” videos to quench the thirst for a good “How was your day?” and *mukbangs* to quench the thirst for a good dinner table conversation.

  

  

Put up a story, and just seeing the list of viewers will make you feel a little bit seen, a little bit more real and, for a moment — a little less lonely, just by the virtue of being observed. ***Observation, even diluted, still registers.***

  

So perhaps our hypothesis holds some value to it. This could be it, right?

  

LHS = RHS?

Hence… tentatively proved.

  

  

At its core: ***observation is proof of existence, and media scales observation.***

***But it also commodifies it.***

  

  

So, in the end of this optimistic derivative, I remind: media may have a noble cause, but its soldiers can still be corrupt.
